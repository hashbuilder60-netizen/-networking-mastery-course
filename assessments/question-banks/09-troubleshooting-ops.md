# Q81-Q90: Troubleshooting and Operations

81. Q: First troubleshooting step?
A: Define impact and scope.

82. Q: Why avoid random command execution?
A: Increases noise and delays root cause.

83. Q: Best data sources for RCA?
A: Logs, counters, flow records, and packet traces.

84. Q: What is blast radius?
A: Scope of impacted systems/users.

85. Q: Why correlate timestamps?
A: Identify trigger sequence accurately.

86. Q: Symptom vs root cause difference?
A: Symptom is observed effect, root cause is underlying trigger.

87. Q: What closes incident properly?
A: Verified restoration plus preventive actions assigned.

88. Q: Why keep troubleshooting journal?
A: Capture patterns and speed future diagnosis.

89. Q: Good escalation includes?
A: Impact, scope, evidence, actions taken.

90. Q: Most important post-incident output?
A: Actionable prevention tasks with owners.
