# Q1-Q10: Fundamentals and Models

1. Q: Why can a host ping local subnet devices but not remote subnets?
A: Default gateway is missing, down, or misconfigured.

2. Q: What does a switch learn in its MAC table?
A: Source MAC to ingress port mapping per VLAN.

3. Q: Which layer determines end-to-end IP addressing?
A: Network layer (Layer 3).

4. Q: What is encapsulation?
A: Adding protocol headers/trailers as data moves down stack.

5. Q: Why is the OSI model useful operationally?
A: It provides structured fault-isolation workflow.

6. Q: Which header changes at every routed hop?
A: Layer 2 frame header.

7. Q: What does successful ping NOT prove?
A: That application ports and services are healthy.

8. Q: Why baseline interface health?
A: To detect abnormal behavior quickly during incidents.

9. Q: What is a broadcast domain?
A: Devices receiving same Layer 2 broadcast frames.

10. Q: First check when all users on one floor are down?
A: Uplink/interface physical and switching state.
